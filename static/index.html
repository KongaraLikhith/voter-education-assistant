📚 Complete Voter Guide

  1. Visit the official portal: voters.eci.gov.in
  2. Click on "New Voter Registration" (Form 6)
  3. Fill in personal details, address, and upload required documents.
  4. Required documents: Aadhaar card, age proof (birth certificate/10th mark sheet), address proof (utility bill/passport), and a recent passport-size photo.
  5. Submit the form and save the reference number to track your status.
  6. Once approved, your Voter ID (EPIC card) will be delivered to your registered address via post.
  • EVM (Electronic Voting Machine): A secure device used to cast votes electronically, replacing paper ballots.
  • How to use: Press the blue button next to the name and symbol of your chosen candidate. A red light will glow, and you will hear a long beep confirming your vote.
  • VVPAT (Voter Verifiable Paper Audit Trail): An independent verification system attached to the EVM.
  • How VVPAT works: After you press the button, a paper slip containing the serial number, name, and symbol of the candidate is printed and remains visible behind a transparent window for 7 seconds before falling into a sealed drop box.
  • Security: EVMs are standalone machines, not connected to the internet, making them immune to online hacking.
  • What it is: A set of guidelines issued by the Election Commission of India for political parties and candidates during elections to ensure free and fair polling.
  • When it applies: It comes into force the moment the election schedule is announced and remains in effect until the results are declared.
  • Prohibitions: Hate speech, bribing voters, using government resources for campaigning, announcing new financial grants, or laying foundation stones of projects.
  • Reporting violations: Citizens can report violations using the cVIGIL app or by calling the 1950 voter helpline.
  • Lok Sabha (General Elections): Citizens vote to elect Members of Parliament (MPs) to the lower house. The party/coalition with a majority (272+ out of 543 seats) forms the Central Government and chooses the Prime Minister. Held every 5 years.
  • State Assembly (Vidhan Sabha): Citizens vote to elect Members of Legislative Assembly (MLAs). The majority forms the State Government and chooses the Chief Minister.
  • Rajya Sabha: The upper house of Parliament. Members are indirectly elected by MLAs, not directly by citizens.
  • Local Body Elections: Elections for Municipal Corporations (urban) and Panchayats (rural).
  • Presidential Election: Indirect election by an electoral college consisting of elected MPs and MLAs.
  • Meaning: "None Of The Above"
  • Introduction: Introduced in 2013 following a Supreme Court order to give voters the right to register a negative opinion.
  • How to vote: It is the last button on the EVM panel, marked with a specific symbol.
  • Impact: NOTA votes are counted but do not affect the election result. Even if NOTA gets the highest votes, the candidate with the next highest votes is declared the winner.
  • Significance: It empowers citizens to send a strong message of dissatisfaction to political parties regarding the quality of candidates fielded.
  • You must carry an approved ID document to cast your vote.
  • Primary ID: Voter ID card (EPIC).
  • Alternative IDs (if you don't have EPIC): Aadhaar Card, PAN Card, Driving License, Indian Passport, MGNREGA Job Card, Passbooks with photo issued by Bank/Post Office, Smart Card issued by RGI under NPR, Health Insurance Smart Card (Ministry of Labour), Pension Document with photo, Official ID cards issued to MPs/MLAs/MLCs, or Service ID Cards with photograph issued by Central/State Govt/PSUs.
  • Note: Voter slips distributed by political parties are not accepted as valid ID. Only the official voter information slip along with one of the above IDs is acceptable.
